 Ears

Cats also express their emotions and intentions with their ears. For example, while forward-facing ears express interest; the more a cat's ears turn to the side and back, the greater the cat's stimulation or distress. 

Backward-facing ears are a sure sign that your cat feels threatened or doesn't like what you're doing. Especially if it's accompanied by a hiss, you might want to leave it alone!

 Eyes 

 Cats communicate with their eyelids (according to how open or closed they are) and the expansion or contraction of the pupil (the black part of the eye). The sudden enlargement of the pupil is caused by sudden arousal, which may be due to fear, interest, or any other strong emotion.

 

 Wide open eyes can signal confidence, while squinting can signal fear or aggression. If your cat is staring at you with drooping, sleepy eyelids, it's a sign that your cat feels comfortable and safe. Besides that, if your cat is looking at another cat without blinking, this is a sign that she is trying to dominate or is aggressive.
